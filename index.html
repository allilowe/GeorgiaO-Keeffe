Georgia O'Keeffe's Life:

  • 1887 Born at a damily dairy farm, near Sun Prairie, Wisconsin.
  • 1892-1990 Attends Town Hall School and receives art lessons at home.
  • 1902 O'Keeffe family move to Williamsburg, Virginia.
  • 1903-1905 attends Chatham Episcopal Institute and serves as art editor of the school yearbook.
  • 1905-1906 attends School of The Art Institute of Chicago and studies with John Vanderpoel.
  • 1907-1908 attends Art Students League, New York studies with WIlliam Merritt Chase, awarded Leadgue's.
  • 1908-1911 moves to Chicago to work as a free-lance commercial artist.
  • 1912-1914 moves to Amarillo, Texas as supervisor of drawing and penmanship in public schools.
  • 1914 enrolls at Teachers College, Columbia University; attends exhbitions of works by Pablo Picasso at 291.
  • 1915 moves to Columbia, South Carolina to teach art at Columbie College; makes decision to chart new direction for her art.
  • 1917 at 291 Georgia's first one-person show opens.
  • 1921 O'keeffe is the star of a show with several nudes at The Anderson Galleries creating public sensation and critics.
  • 1925 Georgia's flower paintings are first exhibited at The Anderson Galleries.
  • 1928 Six of O'Keeffe's lily paintings sell for $25,000.00
  • 1929 Georgia moves to Taos.
  • 1931 The first exhibition with paintings of bones opens at An American Place.
  • 1933 O'Keeffe becomes ill suffering from psychoneurosis.
  • 1934 First visit to Ghost Ranch to help with her 13-month hiatus.
  • 1949 Has multiple shows and travels the country until she leaves New York when her lover Stieglitz dies and she moves to New Mexico.
  • 1951 O'Keeffe tavels to Mexico for six weeks and meets Diego Rivera and Frieda Kahlo.
  • 1965 paints her largest cloud pictures at Ghost Ranch.
  • 1971 loses central vision, only has peripheral sight.
  • 1972 finishes her last unassisted oil painting.
  • 1977 recieves Medal of Freedom from President Gerald Ford.
  • 1985 awarded National Medal of Arts by President Ronald Reagan.
  • 1986 Georgia O'Keeffe dies at St. Vincent Hospital, Santa Fe, New Mexico.
